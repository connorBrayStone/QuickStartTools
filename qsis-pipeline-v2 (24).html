📘 Guidelines

📊 Capacity Overview

This view shows all cohort openings across zones. CICC Resource Coordinators use this to place children who have been identified as potential QS candidates.

🗺️ Zone Assignment

Determine the child's zone based on family address:

Central Zone
Halifax Regional Municipality (HRM), Eastern Shore, West Hants
Eastern Zone
Cape Breton, Antigonish, Guysborough
Western Zone
Annapolis Valley, South Shore, Yarmouth/Southwest — Note: Western requires sub-region selection before placement
Northern Zone
Colchester, Cumberland, Pictou
👶 Age Eligibility

Children must be ≤31 months of age at the cohort start date. A child who would be 32 months or older at cohort start does not meet default age eligibility for that cohort.

The system automatically calculates age at start when placing a child. Red text in the "Age at Start" column indicates the child would exceed 31 months.
🟢 Reading the Capacity Table

General Enrollment — standard openings filled in order. The first empty General row is the next available spot.

HELD openings (orange) — reserved for children who are at risk of aging out of later general openings. Used as a fallback when a child wouldn't meet the age cutoff for the next general spot.

➕ Adding a Child — CICC Triage Process

Follow these steps when adding a child to the capacity sheet:

1
Enter the child's initials (format: First2-Last2-Zone, e.g., JaWa-C) and date of birth.
2
Select the child's zone based on family address. For Western zone, also select the sub-region.
3
Click "Find Best Available Opening". The system will automatically check the next general enrollment opening.
4
Review the result:
• Green (✅) — Child fits in a general opening. Confirm placement.
• Yellow (⚠️) — Child ages out of general. System found a HELD opening. Confirm placement.
• Red (❌) — No eligible opening. Child goes to Waitlist Provisional.
If the result is yellow or red, don't worry — this is the system following the standard triage. HELD openings exist exactly for this purpose, and the Waitlist Provisional triggers a QS supervisor review.
📛 Initials Convention

First 2 letters of first name + First 2 letters of last name + hyphen + Zone letter. Examples: JaWa-C (Central), MiSm-E (Eastern). For hyphenated or compound last names, use first 2 of the primary surname.

⏳ Waitlist Provisional — Supervisor Review

"Waitlist" indicates a child who meets age-eligibility criteria for QS services, but for whom there is currently no enrollment opening available before they would surpass 31 months of age.

📋 Status Definitions
Waitlist — Provisional
CICC admin has placed the family in waitlist status based on the capacity tool — purely based on the child's current age and no available openings prior to surpassing 31 months. This designation is provisional upon review by the QS clinical team. CICC doesn't have visibility into all operational details (held openings being released, scheduling flexibilities, etc.).
Waitlist — Confirmed
The QS clinical team has reviewed the provisional waitlist placement and confirmed that waitlist status is indeed required — there are no operationally workable alternatives. The child remains on the waitlist until an opening becomes available or they reach 32 months (at which point status changes to Ineligible).
Candidate
The QS supervisor identified a workable option and is placing the child back into an enrollment opening. This may involve adding an extra opening to a cohort, using a HELD opening, or applying an age override with expedited session planning.
Ineligible
Child has reached 32 months or older, or another clinical determination has been made that the child does not meet eligibility for QS services at this time.
🔄 Supervisor Review Workflow
1
Review each Provisional entry. Consider: Are there HELD openings being released? Can an additional opening be added to an upcoming cohort? Is an age override workable with expedited sessions?
2
→ Candidate: Opens a placement flow. Select the opening (you may need to add one via Manage Cohorts first). Age override toggle allows placement up to 33 months when expedited sessions are planned.
3
→ Confirmed: No alternatives exist. Add a note explaining the review. Child remains on waitlist.
4
→ Ineligible: Child has aged out entirely or other clinical determination. Add a note with reason.
The provisional/confirmed distinction is about who has reviewed. Think of "Provisional" as the CICC-assigned placeholder, and "Confirmed" as QS clinical verification that no alternatives exist.
⏱️ Timing Considerations

Supervisor review may happen before or after the CICC Resource Coordinator's intake meeting with the family. If the RC meeting and service match occur before supervisor review, the family will appear in the QS pipeline with a "supervisor review required" flag.

📋 Service Match Pipeline

This view tracks families from the moment QS receives a Greenspace service match notification through to QS Intro meeting completion. The pipeline begins after the CICC Resource Coordinator completes their intake meeting and determines QS is a service match.

📨 How Families Enter the Pipeline (A1)
1
CICC Resource Coordinator holds intake meeting with family and determines QS as a service match
2
Family added to QS clinic in Greenspace — notification sent to quickstart@autismns.ca
3
QS Admin reviews child's Greenspace profile: cohort assignment from capacity sheet, operational flag questions, M-CHAT score, RC intake notes
4
Admin enters family into Participation Info and begins contact within 72 hours
🚩 Operational Flags

Flags (⚠️) are checked during Greenspace profile review. If any flag is present, the family is routed to Operational Considerations — Supervisor Review Required before contact begins.

Flag questions: Can caregiver commit to all 19 sessions? · Any extended trips/vacations (>2 weeks) planned? · English workable as primary session language? · Can accommodate QS working hours (Mon–Fri, 8:30am–4:30pm)? · M-CHAT score concerns?

📞 Family Contact Protocol (A2)
1
1st Contact — within 72 hours of service match. Phone or email per preference. Introduce QS, confirm interest, begin scheduling.
2
2nd Contact — if no response. Try alternate method. Leave voicemail with callback number.
3
3rd Contact — final attempt. If no response, consider sending a letter. May result in "Unable to Contact" status.

When contact is made: schedule the QS Intro Meeting 6–8 weeks before cohort start. Send confirmation email with program guide, timeline of sessions, and consent forms.

📊 Status Progression

Assigned from Greenspace → Op. Considerations (if flagged) → 1st/2nd/3rd Attempted Contact → QS Intro Scheduled → QS Intro Complete

Families that cannot be contacted after 3 attempts → Unable to Contact (notify CICC)

📅 QS Intro Meeting (A6)

Supervisor-led meeting, typically 1–2 hours via Zoom or phone. Covers program orientation, consent completion, and discussion-based intake/assessment. The meeting follows a structured template combining information from the OneNote intake guide, PC Intake tab, and consent tracking.

📋 Meeting Preparation
1
Prepare all documents: consent forms, program guide, timeline of sessions, Participation Info (highlight child's row), ShareVision (for safety checklist if home-based)
2
Review child's Greenspace profile — contact notes, consent/guardianship info
3
Create child's SharePoint folder and rename OneNote intake document with child acronym

Tip: Use two screens — one for documents shared with family (consents, parent guide, timeline), one for internal documentation (intake notes, Participation Info).

📝 Meeting Flow
1
Consent Forms — Review all 6 forms (Participation & Confidentiality, Collect/Use/Share, Release/Share, Photo/Video, Videoconferencing, Participation Agreement). DocuSign or hard copy.
2
Program Guide & Scheduling — Review parent guide, timeline of sessions (S1–4 SLP/OT, S5 PC assessment, S6–17 coaching, follow-ups). Confirm session location preference and availability.
3
Participant Info & Background — Household, siblings, medical history, developmental milestones, preferred activities, challenging behaviours, emergency contact.
4
Developmental Domains — ABAs assessment (0–3 rating, domains: Communication, Functional Pre-Academic, Leisure, Self-Care, Social, Motor)
5
COPM — Canadian Occupational Performance Measure. Family priorities for goal-setting.
6
Community Partners & Programs — HSNS, NSECDIS, Diagnostics (IWK/NSH), pediatrician, private SLP/OT, daycare, other services. Verbal consent to connect.
7
Safety Checklist — Pre-visit checklist required if family chooses home-based sessions. Completed via ShareVision during the meeting.
✅ After the Meeting
1
Review and organize intake notes and community partners info for ShareVision upload
2
Upload intake notes as "announcement" on child's ShareVision profile
3
Update Contact Status → Intro Complete; update Parent Coach Intake tab (status & scheduling)
4
Supervisor assigns team (PC, SLP, OT) → Admin schedules Sessions 1–4 → Confirmation email sent to family
✅ Enrollment & Scheduling (A5 / C1)

After QS Intro is complete, the supervisor assigns the family's team and Sessions 1–4 are scheduled. This view will track team assignment, SLP/OT availability coordination, session scheduling, and Parent Coach handoff.

👥 Team Assignment

Supervisor assigns during/right after the QS Intro Meeting:

Parent Coach — stays with the family for Sessions 5–19 (assessment, coaching, follow-ups). Same PC throughout.

SLP & OT — assigned based on zone availability. Part-time schedules vary by zone. Alternating pattern: SLP-OT-SLP-OT or OT-SLP-OT-SLP for Sessions 1–4.

📅 Sessions 1–4 Scheduling

All 4 consultation sessions scheduled at once. Sessions 1–3 default to 9:30am. Session 4 is the handoff session where the Parent Coach joins.

Zone availability (Sessions 1–4):

Central: Mon/Tue · Eastern (Zoom only): Wed/Thu/Fri · Northern: Mon/Wed · Western: varies by sub-region

After scheduling: add to zone Outlook calendar, send confirmation email to family with all 4 dates/times, notify assigned Parent Coach.

🔄 Parent Coach Handoff

After Session 4, the Parent Coach takes ownership of the family's scheduling for Sessions 5–19. The PC provides a full schedule of all remaining sessions. SLP/OT and PC debrief after Sessions 3 and 4.

🔔 Recent Changes

This view provides an audit trail of all additions, removals, and status changes across capacity sheets over the last 7 days. This replaces the Power Automate–driven audit tabs from the Excel capacity sharing tool.

Use this to quickly identify what's changed since your last review, especially after periods when CICC has been actively placing children.

📋 Adding a Family from Service Match

When QS receives a Greenspace service match notification, the QS Admin creates a pipeline entry that links to the child's existing capacity opening — the one already placed by CICC.

🔗 How Capacity Matching Works
1
Enter the child's full name and DOB from Greenspace
2
The system generates an acronym (e.g., "John Smith" → JoSm) and searches capacity openings
3
Matches are found by DOB (strongest) and/or acronym pattern. Zone and cohort are auto-filled.
4
If no match is found, use the manual dropdown to select the correct opening, or verify with the capacity view.
📝 Acronym Format

Standard: First 2 letters of first name + first 2 of last name + zone suffix. Examples:

John Smith (Central) → JoSm-C · Valeria Kline (Northern) → VaKl-N · Kala Smith-Johns (Eastern) → KaSmJo-E

If a 4-letter acronym already exists for another child, a 3rd letter is added from either name.
