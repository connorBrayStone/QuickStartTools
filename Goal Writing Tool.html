📘 How to select a goal target

Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD): The ZPD is the range of skills the child is just starting to develop — skills they can't yet do independently, but can do with help.

Your sweet spot: "P" (Partial) items on the checklist + what the family says they want to work on.

Steps:

1. Look for clusters of "P" items within a developmental level

2. Match emerging skills to COPM / family priorities

3. Check readiness: Is the child showing some signs? Will they have daily opportunities?

5-Part Framework
Build the learning objective using Context, Instructional Cue, Behaviour, Mastery, and Generalization
1. Context
📘 What is Context?

The everyday routines, activities, and settings where the goal will take place. We want children to use new skills naturally throughout their day.

Include: 2–3 example routines using "e.g." to show flexibility. Avoid being too narrow (only one activity) or too broad (every routine listed).

ProblemFix
No context includedAdd examples of routines where skill naturally occurs
Too narrow ("during block play at the table")Broaden: "during everyday play routines (e.g., blocks, pretend play, puzzles)"
Unrealistic for familyCollaborate with family to identify routines they already do
2. Instructional Cue(s)
📘 Restricted vs. Free Operant

Restricted operant: The behaviour needs a specific cue to occur (e.g., someone must call the child's name for the child to respond to their name).

Free operant: The behaviour occurs spontaneously / through child initiation within a general context (e.g., child spontaneously produces single words to comment during play).

If free operant, the Instructional Cue is left blank — the Context serves as the antecedent condition.

3. Behaviour
📘 Writing Strong Target Behaviours

Use active verbs: look, point, give, label, request, respond, shift gaze, imitate, initiate

Avoid vague terms: "understand," "know," "learn," "appropriate," "tolerate," "cooperate"

Ask yourself: Could someone unfamiliar with the child understand exactly what they are expected to do?

ProblemFix
Too vagueAdd more detail about the observable action
Not observableRephrase with visible/audible action
Too complexBreak into simpler teachable unit
⚠️ This may contain vague language. Use observable, measurable terms (e.g., "look," "point," "say") rather than internal states.
4. Mastery
📘 Writing Mastery Criteria

Mastery criteria have three dimensions:

1. Within-session measure: How often (frequency, ratio, duration, latency)

2. Time frame: Per day, per session, per interval, per routine

3. Across-session consistency: PC: 2 consecutive sessions. ESDM: 3 consecutive sessions.

Note: The frequency is the number of times the child is successful, not the number of opportunities presented.

TypeExampleBest for
Frequency/Count5 times per dayQuick, observable skills (gaze, pointing)
Ratio4 out of 5 opportunitiesStructured teaching tasks
DurationFor 3 minutesSustained attention, engagement
LatencyWithin 3 secondsResponse to name, turn-taking
PC: across 2 consecutive sessions. ESDM: across 3 consecutive sessions.
5. Generalization
📘 Planning for Generalization

Generalization = the child can use the skill across different people, routines, materials, and settings.

Not all goals need full generalization in the same phase. Think of it as something you build toward.

Bold & Underlined = always required. Italic = rarely included, only if generalization is unlikely to develop naturally.

TypeExamples
Across PeopleWith Mom, Dad, sibling, daycare staff
Across RoutinesPlaytime, snack, dressing, outdoor time
Across MaterialsDifferent toys, books, snack foods
Across SettingsAt home, in the car, at daycare

📘 Formulating Teaching Steps

Task-analyze the learning objective from current baseline → fully mastered objective.

Teaching steps add a 6th component: Prompts — additional cues that precede and further support evoking the behavior.

What changes across steps:

• Prompts are faded (most → least support)

• Behaviour may become more complex or independent

• Mastery criteria may increase

• Generalization may broaden (typically in later steps)

The final step should match the full learning objective (no prompts, full mastery, full generalization).

What the child is doing now, worded positively. Include what they CAN do in relation to this skill.

Quality Check
Review this goal before finalizing
  • Zone of Proximal Development: Is this skill emerging ("P") or just beyond current level? Not too easy, not too hard.
  • Family Relevance: Does this connect to COPM results and what matters to the family right now?
  • Opportunity: Will the child have enough daily chances to practice this in natural routines?
  • Observable: Can you clearly see or hear when the child does this behaviour?
  • Achievable: Is the mastery criteria realistic for a 3-month period given this child's learning rate?
  • Functional: Will this skill open up new learning opportunities (behaviour cusp)?
