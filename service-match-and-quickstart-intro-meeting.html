Overview

This guide outlines the integrated service match and intro meeting processes between QuickStart Nova Scotia (QSNS) and the Provincial Preschool Autism Service's Clinical Intake and Care Coordination (CICC) team.

Important Context: CICC is the single point of entry for ALL Provincial Preschool Autism Services (PPAS), not just QuickStart. Referrals may come for various reasons (diagnostics, general services, etc.), but CICC should assess QuickStart eligibility for all age-appropriate children.

Integration Soft Launch Date: September 24, 2025
Transition Period: Approximately 1 month (see Archive)

Key Systems and Tools

1. Greenspace Database
- Primary client information system
- Stores demographic, clinical, and program status data

2. Capacity Sharing Excel Sheet
- Located in shared SharePoint folder
- Live tracking of program status and cohort assignments
- Zone-specific tabs with color coding:
- GREEN: Next available general enrollment spot
- ORANGE: HELD spot (for aging-out cases)
- RED: Child over 31 months at start

3. Communication Channels
- Referrals via: Fax, email, or phone
- Follow-up within 72 hours for paper referrals

CICC Admin (Intake Admin) completes:
- Sections 1-4: Initial data entry, zone determination, age-based triage

Status Definitions

Source: QS Supervisor Manual + QSIS application (updated to reflect CICC integration and current terminology). Historically tracked on per-zone Participation Info Excel sheets; being transitioned into QSIS.

Throughout the service match and program participation process, each family's progression is tracked using two summative categories:

  • Contact Status indicates the most recent contact or communication completed in response to a received service match (formerly "referral"). Updated by the QS Admin as contact attempts and scheduling progress.
  • Program Status indicates the determination or current progression of a family's participation in a respective program within the umbrella of QuickStart NS Services. There is a separate program status for each program (PC, ESDM, Behaviour Support, Sleep Support).

Contact Status

Designation Description
Assigned from Greenspace QS Admin has received the service match notification from CICC, reviewed the child's Greenspace profile, and added the child to the participation info sheet. This is the initial contact status when no operational flags were noticed.
Op. Considerations — Supervisor Review Initial status when operational flags were noticed during Greenspace review (Step 9B). QS Admin does not immediately contact the family; instead flags the zone supervisor to review before proceeding.
1st Attempted Contact First attempt to reach the family to schedule the QS Intro Meeting.
2nd Attempted Contact Second attempt to reach the family.
3rd Attempted Contact Third and final attempt to reach the family. If no response after 3rd attempt, status moves to "Unable to Contact."
QS Intro Scheduled QS Intro Meeting has been scheduled with the family.
QS Intro Complete QS Intro Meeting has been completed.
QS Intro Rescheduled A previously scheduled intro meeting was rescheduled.
S1-4 Confirmation Sent Confirmation email sent to the family with finalized team assignment and S1-4 schedule. This is the terminal contact status for families proceeding into the program.
Unable to Contact Family could not be reached after 3 contact attempts. Supervisor and/or manager to be notified.

Note: Historical contact statuses from the pre-CICC process (e.g. "Confirmation of referral sent", "Ineligibility email sent", "ESDM Wait List email sent", "Referral scheduled", "Referral complete") are no longer used. These functions are now handled by CICC during Sections 1-8.

Program Status

These designations apply to each program independently (a family may be "Active" in PC and "Waitlist" for ESDM simultaneously).

Designation Description
Candidate Meets program eligibility criteria, but either: (a) it is undetermined whether available openings exist before the child ages out, or (b) only age-based eligibility has been confirmed so far (other criteria remain to be assessed by CICC/RC). This is the initial Greenspace status set by CICC during capacity sheet triage.
Waitlist Provisional Meets age-based eligibility criteria but no general or held opening is available before turning 32 months. QS Clinical Team is reviewing for operationally workable alternatives. Set by CICC Admin during capacity sheet triage (Section 3B, Scenario E).
Waitlist Confirmed QS Clinical Team has reviewed the waitlist provisional case and determined that no workable alternative arrangements exist. Child remains on the waitlist in case openings become available before they age out. Referral-order priority applies.
Eligible Meets all program eligibility criteria and has been assigned a guaranteed opening for program participation (cohort and start date confirmed). For waitlist cases, this means the QS Clinical Team found a workable arrangement.
Ineligible Does not meet program eligibility criteria. Reasons include: child does not screen in on MCHAT, family declines participation, child has aged out (32+ months at next available start), or other eligibility criteria not met. Reason should be documented.
Active Currently participating in the program. For PC: status changes to Active when the family completes Session 1 (or as early as when S1-4 confirmation emails are sent).
Completed Successfully completed participation in the program. For PC: when a family completes Session 17 (S18-S19 are follow-up sessions, not counted toward completion).
Discontinued Started but did not complete participation in the program. Includes families who withdrew or were discharged due to lack of attendance after starting sessions. See reporting considerations below.

Reporting Considerations for Program Status

The following nuances affect how program status changes are recorded for reporting purposes:

Discontinued — timing matters for reporting:

  • If a family withdraws during or before the intro meeting and there IS sufficient time to find a replacement (e.g. >1 week before cohort start): status may be changed to Discontinued, but no cohort is assigned. This withdrawal is not counted toward reporting metrics.
  • If there is NOT sufficient time to find a replacement (e.g. <1 week before cohort start): the family is considered Discontinued with the cohort assigned, and is counted toward reporting.
  • If a family has started the program (attended S1 or more) and withdraws or is discharged: status is changed to Discontinued and the cohort number remains for reporting.

Ineligible — common scenarios:

  • Child does not screen in on the MCHAT (during RC screening, Section 5)
  • Family chooses not to participate after informed choice discussion (Section 6)
  • Child ages out (reaches 32 months with no available opening and no waitlist resolution)

Participation in QS NS Services

Source: QS Supervisor Manual + QSIS application (Program Management view). Capacity figures derived from QSIS formulas with confirmation from QS Manager, March 2026.

Within QuickStart Nova Scotia's umbrella of services, the Parent Coaching program is the main offered service with the largest total capacity. The ESDM Program is a supportive service to help team members in learning ESDM, and as such is subject to significantly more limited capacities. Participation in QuickStart NS Services entails participation in either the Parent Coaching Program or the ESDM Program.

From an operational perspective, participation is determined by (firstly) reviewing eligibility for the Parent Coach Program and (secondarily) reviewing eligibility for the ESDM Program.

Parent Coaching Program

Participation is primarily determined on a "first-come, first-serve" basis, in accordance with the date of the received referral.

Cohort structure: Organized into cohorts that occur roughly monthly, with 10 total cohorts over the course of a calendar year.

Reasoning for 10 yearly cohorts (instead of 12):
- No cohort is scheduled to start within the month of December
- Over the course of a calendar year, it is anticipated that vacations and other unexpected delays (e.g. staff/family illness) amount in the loss of time/capacity for roughly 1 additional cohort

Capacity and cohort sizing:

Capacity is derived from the number of active Parent Coach FTE in each zone, multiplied by the zone's caseload rate, minus a 0.5 buffer per PC headcount. This produces the formal mandate (yearly families served), which is distributed evenly across 10 cohorts.

Zone Active PC FTE Caseload Rate Op. Max Mandate Cohort Distribution
Central 6.5 (incl. Senior PC at 0.5) 15.5/PC/yr 100 95 10 cohorts of 10
Eastern 3 12.5/PC/yr 37.5 36 6 cohorts of 4 + 4 cohorts of 3
Western 3 12.5/PC/yr 37.5 36 6 cohorts of 4 + 4 cohorts of 3
Northern 2 12.5/PC/yr 25 24 4 cohorts of 3 + 6 cohorts of 2

Formulas: Operational Maximum = Active PC FTE x zone caseload rate. Mandate = Op. Max - (0.5 x PC headcount). These are configurable in QSIS Program Management and auto-adjust when staffing changes.

Sequencing of cohort sizes (which cohorts are larger vs. smaller) is flexible to meet fluctuations in demand, capacity (e.g. reduced team / staff in training), and balancing of caseloads.

Opening assignment — general vs. held:

Within each cohort, the majority of openings are assigned on a "first-come, first-serve" basis. A portion of openings are designated as HELD to provide flexibility in accommodating received referrals where a child may be nearing 31 months of age and where no openings are available before the child would surpass 31 months.

Typical HELD openings per cohort: Central ~2 per cohort, Provincial zones 0-2 per cohort depending on cohort size.

Process for assigning held openings:

  1. Assign to the NEXT child (by order of received referral) who would otherwise be age-ineligible for the PC program if they don't participate in that cohort
  2. If no children are currently in that circumstance, continue to hold the opening in that cohort in the event of a subsequent referral meeting that criterion
  3. Once it is time to move ahead with intro meetings (~6 weeks prior) for the cohort and the held opening has not been filled, contact the next child on the list (in order of received referral) to inquire if they would like to take that spot and start earlier — continue until the spot is filled

Average waiting times:

The Parent Coaching Program aims to maintain an average waiting time of no longer than 2-3 months between the date when it is determined that a child meets the eligibility requirements (during the referral meeting) and the earliest available starting date.

With this goal:
- All children who meet eligibility requirements and are no older than 29 months at the time of the referral meeting should ideally be provided with a guaranteed opportunity to participate (i.e. a projected cohort assignment)
- For children who meet eligibility requirements and are between 30-31 months, the aim is to provide as many as possible with a guaranteed opportunity. The withheld openings are designated for this purpose. Even with this measure, there may be children in this category where it is not possible to provide a guaranteed opportunity — in these instances, the child would be placed on a "Wait List" for the PC program, in the event that a child with a projected cohort assignment withdraws. Wait List children are contacted on a "first-come, first-serve" basis (date of received referral).

ESDM Program

Also primarily determined on a "first-come, first-serve" basis in accordance with the date of the received referral.

The ESDM program is subject to more limited capacities compared to the Parent Coaching Program, and it is anticipated that it will not be possible to offer participation to all referred families who meet eligibility requirements. This is not a main service and is used to support the team in learning ESDM.

Yearly capacities:

Zone ESDM/PC/Year Caseload Note
Central 1 Trial: 1 fewer ESDM, 2 more PC due to shorter travel distances
Eastern 2 Standard non-central rate
Western 2 Standard non-central rate
Northern 2 Standard non-central rate

ESDM slot equivalency: 1 ESDM family = 2 PC "slots".

Supervisors, SLPs, and OTs are assigned ESDM Program participants as needed for acquisition and maintenance of ESDM Fidelity.

Organization of ESDM capacity — Central:

Participation is sub-divided based on the earliest available opening within age brackets:
- Children 30-34 months (ineligible for PC based on earliest possible starting time): 4 spots yearly
- Children 35-39 months: 4 spots yearly
- Children 40-45 months: 4 spots yearly

Target onboarding rate: ~1 ESDM family monthly (across the whole Central team). Monthly openings alternate between each of the 3 age brackets (e.g., August: 30-34mo, September: 35-39mo, October: 40-45mo, November: 30-34mo, etc.).

Refer to ESDM Program - Waitlist for more background on this selection process.

Organization of ESDM capacity — Provincial (Eastern / Western / Northern):

Participation is fully determined on a "first-come, first-serve" basis. Unlike Central, age brackets are not applied at this time. Target onboarding rate: 4-6 ESDM families yearly per zone, 2 ESDM families yearly per Parent Coach.

Supervisor Expectations

Community outreach:

It is the responsibility of the QuickStart NS Supervisor for each zone to ensure that community outreach has occurred and is maintained over time with the relevant professionals and services in that zone who are also working with children and families that could potentially benefit from QuickStart NS Services. This may include a combination of distribution of the FAQ and other general information sheets as well as direct presentation to other professionals and services in the community.

Monitoring volume of service matches:

Through community outreach, it is the responsibility of the supervisor to facilitate that a sufficient number of families are being referred to PPAS (and subsequently service-matched to QuickStart NS) to continue to fill the capacities for the programs being offered. The supervisor should continue to monitor and be aware of the volume of received service matches at any given time — a lower volume or decrease in service matches may indicate the need to re-initiate or increase community outreach.

Part 2: Complete Age-Based Triage Using Capacity Sharing Sheet (CICC Admin, QS Clinical Team for waitlist provisional cases)

The CICC Admin opens the Capacity Sharing Sheet in Excel Online (recommended over desktop to avoid version conflicts and accidental sheet locking). Full step-by-step instructions are maintained in the Guidelines tab of the workbook. Summary of the procedure:

A) Navigate to the appropriate zone tab (Central, Eastern, Western, or Northern).

Western Zone only: Before proceeding, use the dropdown in cell D2 (highlighted yellow) to select the correct sub-region (Southwest, South Shore, or Annapolis Valley).

B) Locate the GREEN highlighted row — this is the next available opening for general enrollment.

C) Enter the child's initials in Column E and date of birth in Column F.

Child initials convention: first two letters of first name / first two letters of last name / zone initial. Example: "Jacob Wallace" in Central → JaWa-C. Date of birth: use the date selector popup when available; if entering manually, use MM/DD/YYYY format exactly.

D) Check Column H (child's age at the cohort start date):

  • Scenario 1 — No red in Column H: Child fits in this opening. Keep child in this row, mark "Candidate" as Parent Coach Status in Greenspace, continue with RC scheduling.
  • Scenario 2 — Red in Column H: Child will be over 31 months at this cohort's start date. Copy the child's acronym and DOB from columns E & F, paste into the next closest ORANGE (HELD) row upward. Delete from the original row. Check Column H again in the new row. Repeat upward through HELD openings until finding one with no red (child will still be under 32 months).

E) If no HELD opening works (all ORANGE rows still show red in Column H):

  1. Remove the child's initials and DOB from the capacity sheet (important — do not leave them in a non-viable row)
  2. Copy the child's information to the "Waitlist Provisional" tab (follow instructions at top of that tab)
  3. Mark "Waitlist Provisional" in Greenspace
  4. Continue with RC scheduling (do not pause — the RC call still occurs)

After this point, the QS Clinical Team reviews waitlist provisional cases per Section 3.5.

Sheet protection note: The capacity sharing sheet is protected in all areas except Columns E, F, and I of each zone tab. This prevents accidental changes to formulas and conditional formatting. If changes beyond those columns are required, contact CICC or QS Manager.

Section 3.5: Review of Waitlist Provisional Cases

Responsible: QS Clinical Team (Zone Supervisor, with Director/Manager support as needed)

When Does This Apply?

When a child has no general enrollment or held opening available before turning 32 months of age.

What Happens?

CICC Admin adds the child to the waitlist provisional tab (per Section 2E in the guidelines tab) while QS Clinical team reviews for operational flexibility that may allow program participation.

Why review at this stage? Advanced awareness allows the QS team to anticipate time-sensitive arrangements that may be less feasible if flagged only after service matching.

QS Clinical Team Review Process

Notification method: Daily email at 7:00 AM lists changes to capacity sharing sheets, including new waitlist provisional cases.

Available information at this stage:
- Child acronym, zone, date of birth only (family has not yet consented to information sharing)
- Full Greenspace profile becomes available after RC meeting if family is a service match

Steps:

  1. Monitor the waitlist provisional tab for children in your zone(s)
  2. Assess if workable operational arrangements exist to allow participation before 32 months
  3. Plan (optional):
  4. Use hidden columns A-D for preliminary mapping (cohort #, start time, notes). Remember to re-hide when done.
  5. If considering an available/unfilled opening already on the zone's tab, put HOLD in the "CHILD acronym" column for that opening so it doesn't get filled while you consolidate your review of workable alternatives.
  6. Document your consolidated decision in Column I (all cases) & zone tab (if opening was found):

If opening identified:

Reviewed by [supervisor name] - proceed with parent coach status as "candidate". Added to [ZONE TAB - cohort & opening #]

Then add child's acronym and DOB to the identified opening in the zone tab.

If no opening identified:

Reviewed by [supervisor name] - no openings identified, proceed with parent coach status as "waitlist confirmed".

CICC Follow-Up Actions

Before RC call: RC checks Column I for additional notes when preparing for families with "waitlist provisional" status.

During RC call: RC discusses QS based on:
- Notes present: Updated parent coach status from additional notes
- No notes present: Waitlist status (QS team hasn't completed review yet)

After RC call:

Outcome Action
Service match to QS RC proceeds per Sections 4+ guidelines
Not a service match RC deletes child from waitlist provisional tab

Deletion serves as communication to QS team that RC call occurred and child was not a service match (since ineligible children don't appear in QS Greenspace clinic).

Step 9D: Contact Family to Schedule Intro Meeting

Initial contact method: Email (see Email Template 1: Initial Contact). Email is sent within 72 hours of receiving the service match. Email typically has a high response rate.

If no response within 2 business days: Follow up by phone. If unable to reach by phone, continue following the 3-attempt contact policy (update contact status in the participation info sheet after each attempt).

3-Attempt Contact Policy:

  1. 1st Attempted Contact — initial email sent to family with intro meeting options (see Email Template 1). Wait at least 2 business days for a response.
  2. 2nd Attempted Contact — follow-up phone call. If no answer, leave a voicemail. If no phone number available, send a follow-up email. Wait at least 2 business days.
  3. 3rd Attempted Contact — final phone call. If no answer:
  4. Follow up with CICC / the referring source to notify them that the family could not be reached
  5. Send a final email to the family indicating QS will no longer be reaching out, and to contact QS if they are still interested
  6. Update contact status to "Unable to Contact" and notify the zone supervisor

Within the participation info sheet, add a short record of all contact attempts (call or email, with dates) as a comment/note within the cell containing that child's initials.

NOTE: The historical contact policy was phone-first (reflecting direct referrals). Current practice following CICC integration is email-first, as the initial email (Template 1) typically has a high response rate and families have already spoken with the CICC Resource Coordinator. The 2-business-day wait and 3-attempt structure remain the same.

Receipt and Transfer of Referrals to CICC (Transition Period)

Responsible: QS Admin

The following outlines the steps that were required for receipt and transfer of referrals to CICC only during the process launch phase of intake integration (starting September 24, 2025), and only pertaining to received referrals where the child was under 32 months of age.

What remained the same during transition:
- QuickStart continued to receive referrals directly through the referral form on the QuickStart website (Microsoft Form that auto-populates an Excel sheet)
- Referral Excel sheet checked twice daily on all working days (start of day and mid-day)
- Double-submission process unchanged: second referral not processed unless differing information; connect with QS NS Manager if needed

What changed (processing received referrals):
- No longer sending an email to families regarding receiving their referral (CICC handles this)
- New referrals processed one at a time, oldest to newest, with the following steps per referral:
1. Copy the full row from the Excel sheet and paste into the QuickStart NS Referral Information Formatter
2. Click "Convert to Document"
3. Verify accuracy of converted document
4. Save as PDF: naming convention "QuickStart NS Referral CHILD ACRONYM"
5. Open IWK's secure email client (MOVEit)
6. Click "Go to Mailbox", then "Send Package"
7. Compose email:
- To: referrals-preschoolautism@iwk.nshealth.ca
- Subject: QuickStart NS Referral CHILD ACRONYM
- Body: "Hello, Please find the attached referral"
8. Attach the PDF and send
9. Delete the PDF from your computer after sending
10. On the ANS referral form Excel sheet, put the date you sent the email to CICC in row AG

NOTE: Children age-ineligible for parent coaching but still under 45 months (e.g. ESDM program waitlist) continued as usual -- add to "Referral and Eligibility" tab. The "Referral and Eligibility" tab was generally no longer used for children under 32 months, but for children 32 months and over, processes continued as usual starting with that tab.

Consent Withdrawal Handling (Transition Period)

If referral form was submitted without consent to transfer to CICC:
- Admins to track forms submitted but not completed due to withdrawal of consent
- If contact information was provided:
- Follow up within 24 hours to complete the QS NS Referral form word document with them
- Connect with QS NS Supervisor in the zone pertaining to the child; Supervisor to connect with CICC team to arrange cohort assignment

If phone call received from family inquiring about referring to QuickStart:
- Ask: "Did you complete or try to complete the referral form on our website?"
- If yes: "Did the referral form ask you to call us directly?"
- If yes: confirm withdrawal of consent with IWK and CICC team
- If they confirmed they did not want to consent: complete the QS NS Referral form word document with them; connect with zone Supervisor who connects with CICC for cohort assignment
- If no: redirect to complete the referral form
