Formerly: "Referral and Intake"
Last updated: April 2, 2026
Context: Following integration with the Provincial Preschool Autism Service's Clinical Intake and Care Coordination (CICC) team, the majority of referral and intake steps (Sections 1-8) are now performed by CICC. Overview Core

This guide outlines the integrated service match and intro meeting processes between QuickStart Nova Scotia (QSNS) and the Provincial Preschool Autism Service's Clinical Intake and Care Coordination (CICC) team.

Important Context: CICC is the single point of entry for ALL Provincial Preschool Autism Services (PPAS), not just QuickStart. Referrals may come for various reasons (diagnostics, general services, etc.), but CICC should assess QuickStart eligibility for all age-appropriate children.

Integration Soft Launch Date: September 24, 2025
Transition Period: Approximately 1 month (see Archive)

Status Definitions Core

Source: QS Supervisor Manual + QSIS application (updated to reflect CICC integration and current terminology). Historically tracked on per-zone Participation Info workbooks; being transitioned into QSIS.

Throughout the service match and program participation process, each family's progression is tracked using two summative categories:

  • Contact Status indicates the most recent contact or communication completed in response to a received service match (formerly "referral"). Updated by the QS Admin as contact attempts and scheduling progress.
  • Program Status indicates the determination or current progression of a family's participation in a respective program within the umbrella of QuickStart NS Services. There is a separate program status for each program (PC, ESDM, Behaviour Support, Sleep Support).

Understanding the Waitlist: Matched vs. Not, Provisional vs. Confirmed Waitlist

"Waitlist" carries two independent distinctions. Keeping them separate prevents the most common QS-team mix-up — reading "waitlist confirmed" as "matched."

Axis Question it answers Where it lives Values
Match (the pivotal QS distinction) Has the family been matched to QS and accepted? Participation Info workbook → Referral & Eligibility tab (post-consent) Matched (on the tab, status "Waitlist") vs not yet matched (not on the tab)
Operational review (CICC phase) Has QS reviewed CICC's by-the-book placement for operational flexibility? Capacity Sharing Sheet → waitlist-provisional tab (pre-consent; acronym/DOB only) Provisional (not yet reviewed) → Confirmed (reviewed, no flexibility), or resolved into an opening

"Provisional / confirmed" belong to the operational-review axis only — they say nothing about whether the family is matched. The two axes are independent: a child can be confirmed but not matched (the RC meeting hasn't happened, or the family declined), or matched but still provisional (the supervisor didn't get to review before the RC meeting).

Key consequences:

  • The QS waitlist is one list: children on the Referral & Eligibility tab with status "Waitlist" — recorded in the Parent Coaching Status (PC STATUS) column, never the ESDM Status column (see Step 9B). Being on that tab is the match. There is no separate "confirmed" list.
  • You cannot read match status off the capacity sheet's provisional tab — it mixes matched and not-yet-matched children, and (pre-consent) QS cannot tell which is which, or whether an unmatched child's RC meeting simply hasn't happened yet.
  • "Waitlist confirmed" ≠ "matched." If you mean "matched and on our waitlist," say "on the QS waitlist."
  • The GreenSpace prov./confirmed value seeds the QS contact status at transfer: when the QS admin adds a waitlisted match to the Referral & Eligibility tab, GreenSpace "Waitlist provisional" → contact status "operational considerations – supervisor review required" (review still owed); GreenSpace "Waitlist confirmed" → "assigned from Greenspace" (review already done). Either way the program status is "Waitlist."

See Step 9B for how a matched child is recorded on the waitlist (the single-waitlist convention and tab columns), Section 3.5 for the provisional → confirmed review process (the operational-review axis), and Working the Waitlist When an Opening Arises for how the supervisor works the waitlist (age highlighting) when openings come up. Reporting Considerations for Program Status

The following nuances affect how program status changes are recorded for reporting purposes:

Discontinued — timing matters for reporting:

  • If a family withdraws during or before the intro meeting and there IS sufficient time to find a replacement (e.g. >1 week before cohort start): status may be changed to Discontinued, but no cohort is assigned. This withdrawal is not counted toward reporting metrics.
  • If there is NOT sufficient time to find a replacement (e.g. <1 week before cohort start): the family is considered Discontinued with the cohort assigned, and is counted toward reporting.
  • If a family has started the program (attended S1 or more) and withdraws or is discharged: status is changed to Discontinued and the cohort number remains for reporting.

Ineligible — common scenarios:

  • Child does not screen in on the MCHAT (during RC screening, Section 5)
  • Family chooses not to participate after informed choice discussion (Section 6)
  • Child ages out (reaches 37 months on the waitlist with no Parent Coaching opening or ESDM spot offered)

A single "Waitlist" status covers both streams. There is no separate ESDM waitlist status — the same "Waitlist" value applies whether a child is waiting for a Parent Coaching or an ESDM opening. See the GreenSpace fields note above and Scenario 3.

Participation in QS NS Services Core

Source: QS Supervisor Manual + QSIS application (Program Management view). Capacity figures derived from QSIS formulas with confirmation from QS Manager, March 2026.

Within QuickStart Nova Scotia's umbrella of services, the Parent Coaching program is the main offered service with the largest total capacity. The ESDM Program is a supportive service to help team members in learning ESDM, and as such is subject to significantly more limited capacities. Participation in QuickStart NS Services entails participation in either the Parent Coaching Program or the ESDM Program.

From an operational perspective, participation is determined by (firstly) reviewing eligibility for the Parent Coach Program and (secondarily) reviewing eligibility for the ESDM Program.

Parent Coaching Program Waitlist

Participation is primarily determined on a "first-come, first-serve" basis, in accordance with the date of the received service match.

Cohort structure: Organized into cohorts that occur roughly monthly, with 10 total cohorts over the course of a calendar year.

Reasoning for 10 yearly cohorts (instead of 12):
- No cohort is scheduled to start within the month of December
- Over the course of a calendar year, it is anticipated that vacations and other unexpected delays (e.g. staff/family illness) amount in the loss of time/capacity for roughly 1 additional cohort

Capacity and cohort sizing:

Capacity is derived from the number of active Parent Coach FTE in each zone, multiplied by the zone's caseload rate, minus a 0.5 buffer per PC headcount. This produces the formal mandate (yearly families served), which is distributed evenly across 10 cohorts.

Zone Active PC FTE Caseload Rate Op. Max Mandate Cohort Distribution
Central 6.5 (incl. Senior PC at 0.5) 15.5/PC/yr 100 95 10 cohorts of 10
Eastern 3 12.5/PC/yr 37.5 36 6 cohorts of 4 + 4 cohorts of 3
Western 3 12.5/PC/yr 37.5 36 6 cohorts of 4 + 4 cohorts of 3
Northern 2 12.5/PC/yr 25 24 4 cohorts of 3 + 6 cohorts of 2

Formulas: Operational Maximum = Active PC FTE x zone caseload rate. Mandate = Op. Max - (0.5 x PC headcount). These are configurable in QSIS Program Management and auto-adjust when staffing changes.

Sequencing of cohort sizes (which cohorts are larger vs. smaller) is flexible to meet fluctuations in demand, capacity (e.g. reduced team / staff in training), and balancing of caseloads.

Opening assignment — general vs. held:

Within each cohort, the majority of openings are assigned on a "first-come, first-serve" basis. A portion of openings are designated as HELD to provide flexibility in accommodating received referrals where a child may be nearing 32 months of age and where no openings are available before the child would surpass 32 months.

Typical HELD openings per cohort: Central ~2 per cohort, Provincial zones 0-2 per cohort depending on cohort size.

Process for assigning held openings:

  1. Assign to the NEXT child (by order of received service match) who would otherwise be age-ineligible for the PC program if they don't participate in that cohort
  2. If no children are currently in that circumstance, continue to hold the opening in that cohort in the event of a subsequent referral meeting that criterion
  3. Once it is time to move ahead with intro meetings (~6 weeks prior) for the cohort and the held opening has not been filled, contact the next child on the list (in order of received service match) to inquire if they would like to take that spot and start earlier — continue until the spot is filled

Average waiting times:

The Parent Coaching Program's ideal is an average waiting time of no longer than 2-3 months between the point a child is determined eligible and the earliest available start date. This remains the target range we aim not to exceed — but it is an ideal rather than the current reality: waiting times are presently well over this across all zones, and there are no immediate capacity-increase options available to close the gap.

For any child who is under 32 months of age at the time the PPAS referral is received by the CICC team, the aim is to provide as many as possible with a guaranteed opportunity to participate (i.e. a projected cohort assignment). The withheld (HELD) openings are designated for this purpose. Even with this measure, there may be children for whom it is not possible to provide a guaranteed opportunity — in these instances the child is placed on the QS waitlist (see Understanding the Waitlist) and is contacted when an opening becomes available, on a "first-come, first-serve" basis by date of received service match (see Working the Waitlist When an Opening Arises).

ESDM Program ESDM Waitlist

ESDM access via the QS waitlist (as of June 15, 2026 — ESDM integration). ESDM is no longer accessed through a separate ESDM intake/waitlist. CICC supports ESDM referrals through the same QuickStart NS waitlist as Parent Coaching: children referred under 32 months are first considered for PC, and those who cannot access a PC opening remain on the waitlist where they may be offered a limited ESDM spot up to 37 months of age (one waitlist, either stream; families access only one stream). When an ESDM spot opens, QS staff identify eligible children from the waitlist by referral order and contact families directly. Children already on the legacy ESDM waitlist continue to be prioritized until that list is fully served.

Also primarily determined on a "first-come, first-serve" basis in accordance with the date of the received service match.

The ESDM program is subject to more limited capacities compared to the Parent Coaching Program, and it is anticipated that it will not be possible to offer participation to all referred families who meet eligibility requirements. This is not a main service and is used to support the team in learning ESDM.

ESDM slot equivalency: 1 ESDM family = 2 PC "slots".

Supervisors, SLPs, and OTs are assigned ESDM Program participants as needed for acquisition and maintenance of ESDM Fidelity.

Selection from the waitlist:

ESDM has no age brackets — enrollment is filled from the same zone waitlist as Parent Coaching, in order of date of received service match (first-come, first-serve), with ESDM's wider age ceiling allowing participation up to 36 months 31 days. Because a family may access only one stream, a child offered and enrolled in one stream is removed from waitlist consideration for the other. For how the supervisor works the waitlist when an ESDM (or PC) opening arises — including the age highlighting — see Working the Waitlist When an Opening Arises.

Target onboarding rate: roughly 1 ESDM family monthly across the Central team; 4–6 ESDM families yearly per provincial zone (≈2 per Parent Coach).

Part 2: Complete Age-Based Triage Using Capacity Sharing Sheet (CICC Admin, with QS Supervisor review for waitlist provisional cases)

Formal rule (CICC) vs. functional QS threshold. This capacity-sheet triage applies the formal under-32-months rule strictly — a child who cannot be fit into a Parent Coaching opening before turning 32 months goes on the waitlist-provisional tab. On review, the QS Clinical Team extends Parent Coaching eligibility to ≤33 months 15 days (see Section 3.5), so some children waitlisted here at 32–33m15d are later slotted into PC by QS. CICC keeps the formal rule; the ≤33m15d threshold is QS-internal only.

The CICC Admin opens the Capacity Sharing Sheet in Excel Online (recommended over desktop to avoid version conflicts and accidental sheet locking). Full step-by-step instructions are maintained in the Guidelines tab of the workbook. Summary of the procedure:

A) Navigate to the appropriate zone tab (Central, Eastern, Western, or Northern).

Western Zone only: Before proceeding, use the dropdown in cell D2 (highlighted yellow) to select the correct sub-region (Southwest, South Shore, or Annapolis Valley).

B) Locate the GREEN highlighted row — this is the next available opening for general enrollment.

C) Enter the child's initials in Column E and date of birth in Column F.

Child initials convention: first two letters of first name / first two letters of last name / zone initial. Example: "Jacob Wallace" in Central → JaWa-C. Date of birth: use the date selector popup when available; if entering manually, use MM/DD/YYYY format exactly.

D) Check Column H (child's age at the cohort start date):

  • Scenario 1 — No red in Column H: Child fits in this opening. Keep child in this row, mark "Candidate" as the Program Status in Greenspace, continue with RC scheduling.
  • Scenario 2 — Red in Column H: Child will be 32 months or older at this cohort's start date. Copy the child's acronym and DOB from columns E & F, paste into the next closest ORANGE (HELD) row upward. Delete from the original row. Check Column H again in the new row. Repeat upward through HELD openings until finding one with no red (child will still be under 32 months).

E) If no HELD opening works (all ORANGE rows still show red in Column H):

  1. Remove the child's initials and DOB from the capacity sheet (important — do not leave them in a non-viable row)
  2. Copy the child's information to the "Waitlist Provisional" tab (follow instructions at top of that tab)
  3. Mark "Waitlist Provisional" in Greenspace
  4. Continue with RC scheduling (do not pause — the RC call still occurs)

After this point, the QS Clinical Team reviews waitlist provisional cases per Section 3.5.

Sheet protection note: The capacity sharing sheet is protected in all areas except Columns E, F, and I of each zone tab. This prevents accidental changes to formulas and conditional formatting. If changes beyond those columns are required, contact CICC or QS Manager.

CICC is responsible for day-to-day data entry as they meet with families, and uses the sheet to hold service-matching conversations. The QS team carries deeper operational knowledge of program capacity and enrollment guidelines that doesn't fully translate into the simplified shared-sheet format. Ongoing supervisor monitoring closes that gap — catching entry inconsistencies early, when they're easy to correct, before they affect a family's match or a cohort's start.

What to monitor. Each zone supervisor reviews the capacity sharing sheet for their zone(s) on an ongoing basis. A daily 7:00 AM email digest of changes to the capacity sharing sheets (additions, removals across all tabs) is sent to all supervisors and the QS Manager, and serves as a useful starting point — though not a complete substitute for direct review. Supervisors are also encouraged to develop their own forward-looking monitoring routines on top of the baseline (e.g., proactively flagging upcoming cohorts where openings remain unfilled at the trigger threshold described below).

Specific focus areas:

Monitor the following within the capacity sharing sheet:

  • Zone allocation accuracy: Children appearing in the correct zone tab. (E.g., a Central child should not appear in the Northern tab.) Flag and re-route any cross-zone entries with CICC for correction.
  • Cohort openings match expected structure: The available openings in the sheet should match what's expected for the next enrolling cohort(s). Raise any structural discrepancies (missing rows, mis-sequenced cohort numbers, etc.) with the QS Manager.
  • Duplicate or ambiguous acronyms: Two children with the same acronym in the same zone need disambiguation.
  • Missing matches as cohorts approach (~8 weeks pre-start trigger): QuickStart intro meetings are conducted approximately 4-6 weeks before a family's actual start date (see Step 9D), and require lead time to receive the match, contact the family, and schedule the meeting. The practical floor is therefore approximately 8 weeks pre-cohort: if a match for an upcoming cohort opening hasn't been received by then, send a general inquiry email to PPAS Intake regarding any pending matches and the expected timeline for the intake meeting. The same applies if entries on the sheet appear to need consolidation or removal but haven't been (e.g., a child entry that should have been moved to Waitlist Provisional, or removed entirely after being matched elsewhere).
  • Waitlist Provisional cases (highest-priority monitoring): New entries on the Waitlist Provisional tab require timely QS Clinical Team review per Section 3.5. The 7:00 AM email digest flags these.

Where supervisor responsibility ends. Direct data entry into the capacity sharing sheet remains CICC's responsibility for incoming families. The supervisor's role here is permanent-product review and quality assurance — flagging issues to CICC for correction, not making corrections directly. The exception is the Waitlist Provisional zone-tab updates, which the QS Clinical Team owns per Section 3.5 (e.g., adding the child's acronym and DOB to an identified opening once a workable cohort fit is found).

Contacting the Family

3-Attempt Contact Policy:

Initial outreach is via email within 72 hours of receiving the service match (see Email Template 1: Initial Contact) — email typically has a high response rate. For each attempt, if successful (intro meeting time agreed) → proceed to "Upon scheduling" steps below.

  1. 1st Attempted Contact — initial email sent to family with intro meeting options (see Email Template 1). Wait at least 2 business days for a response.
  2. 2nd Attempted Contact — follow-up phone call. If no answer, leave a voicemail. If no phone number available, send a follow-up email. Wait at least 2 business days.
  3. 3rd Attempted Contact — final phone call.

If unable to contact after the 3rd attempt:

  • Leave the contact status at "3rd attempted contact" (do not yet update to "Notified - unable to contact" — the supervisor will handle that after their closeout review)
  • Add a note in the Participation Info workbook tagging the zone supervisor that 3 contact attempts have been made unsuccessfully
  • Hand off to the supervisor for closeout per Step 9F

Scheduling the Intro Meeting

Intro meeting timing: Aim to schedule intro meetings within a window of 4-6 weeks before the cohort start date. Ideal is 6 weeks out; 5 weeks is fine; 4 weeks is acceptable if necessary. Meeting duration: 1.5 hours has consistently proven sufficient (reduced from the original 2-hour estimate).

Scheduling considerations (QS Admin reviews supervisor calendar):

  • Generally 1 intro meeting per day per supervisor is a comfortable rate. 2 per day can work; 3 is pushing it but may be done when a cohort needs to be finalized quickly.
  • Check the supervisor's calendar for travel days or out-of-office sessions — avoid scheduling intro meetings on those days.
  • Leave buffer time around intro meeting slots — avoid scheduling immediately adjacent to other events in the supervisor's calendar, especially events that require the supervisor to be out of office (travel time needs to be factored in).

Key facts:

  • Validity: 1 year from date of signing.

1. Coordination on HSNS-Staffed SLP Services PPAS

Background — Historical vs Current Administration

Communication-focused (SLP) support delivered by Hearing and Speech Nova Scotia (HSNS) staff is likely the most realistic next-tier PPAS service available to QS families either concurrent with QS participation or immediately after it. CICC integration changed the administrative pathways for accessing these services without changing the services themselves — and that change introduced new navigation considerations for QS staff.

Historical state (pre-CICC integration). HSNS administered two SLP service streams internally:

  • Core Pediatric SLP — open to all children, including those with suspected (pre-diagnosis) autism. Referrals placed directly with HSNS.
  • Core Autism SLP — for children with a confirmed autism diagnosis. Also administered internally within HSNS.

Transitions between caseloads (e.g., when a child received a diagnosis) were managed and administered internally within HSNS. From the QS team's perspective, one question sufficed: "Is the family connected with HSNS for SLP support?" The internal administrative routing was not visible or actionable for QS staff or families.

Current state (post-CICC integration):

  1. Core Pediatric SLP remains unchanged — direct referral to HSNS, open to children including those with suspected autism pre-diagnosis. HSNS continues to administer internally.
  2. Core Autism SLP has phased out and PPAS SLP support within the Communication Services Focused Intervention Pathway has phased in. The services themselves are still staffed by HSNS, but access is now administered by CICC rather than by HSNS internally.

The two streams are now administratively distinct, and the route to access each differs. Knowing which stream applies to a given family, what's involved in transitioning between them, and what access gaps can occur is now part of the QS team's care-coordination work.

Slip scenarios to watch for

Scenario A — Undiagnosed → diagnosed mid-program. Family started on the Core Pediatric SLP pathway via direct HSNS referral pre-diagnosis; the appropriate access route shifts mid-program once a diagnosis is in place. Largely a CICC/HSNS-internal administrative issue, but families may experience confusion and benefit from QS-side clarification.

Scenario B — Paused SLP support during QS. QS policy does not require families to stop concurrent services. Some families choose to pause SLP support for capacity reasons during QS. CICC will not proactively re-initiate Communication Services for these families at end of service. This is the most common slip and the one QS most directly carries responsibility for surfacing.

Implications for conversation

For most situations, this is a regular care-coordination touchpoint — tracking, asking, sharing information — using the established care-coordination practice covered in the existing QS and Supervisor Manuals.

What's specific to surface (PPAS-driven):

  • For families with a diagnosis on the PPAS Communication Services pathway: confirm awareness that access is now administered through CICC, not directly through HSNS.
  • For Scenario B families especially: prime the message that they may need to actively re-initiate contact with CICC for Communication Services as QS ends — "in your case, given you paused communication services, there may be a specific reason to call CICC back when QS is wrapping up." The same tailored prompting is carried forward in the closing-phase conversation; see Section 13 — Reference: Family-Facing Scripts.

Reference: Guardrails — What NOT to Do or Say

To see details of this reference, switch to Full view.

End-of-service conversations and follow-up actions have specific guardrails — both operational don't-do's and conversational phrases to avoid. Keeping these clear protects families from misinformation and protects QS from setting unrealistic expectations.

Operational — Do NOT:

  • Connect families retroactively with CICC. Only inform and offer connection to families who ended QS services on or after December 1, 2025. Families whose services ended prior to that date were contacted once via a time-limited informational outreach in late 2025 and should not be re-engaged proactively by QS today (see Archive: End-of-Service Outreach — Pre-Dec 2025 Families for context).
  • Share Discharge or Completion Reports for children not actively supported by QS. The report must reflect current QS programming or a discharge that just occurred. Do not share stale reports.
  • Proceed without explicit family consent. Always ask and document the family's willingness to share reports or have referrals placed on their behalf.

Conversational — Avoid these phrases:

  • "CICC will diagnose your child" — CICC is coordination/consultation; diagnosis comes from PPAS Autism Diagnosis Services.
  • "If you get a referral, you're guaranteed a spot in [service]" — referral does not equal placement. Eligibility and capacity determine enrollment.
  • "QuickStart will automatically transition you to the next service" — QS ends; PPAS is a separate system. Family must engage.
  • "CICC will reach out to you within X weeks" — don't promise timelines. Services are coordinated based on need and capacity, not on a fixed schedule.
  • "You need to reapply or retell your story to CICC" — if a referral is already placed, CICC has the information. Reassure that the transition is handled.

Receipt and Transfer of Referrals to CICC (Transition Period)

Responsible: QS Admin

The following outlines the steps that were required for receipt and transfer of referrals to CICC only during the process launch phase of intake integration (starting September 24, 2025), and only pertaining to received referrals where the child was under 32 months of age.

What remained the same during transition:
- QuickStart continued to receive referrals directly through the referral form on the QuickStart website. The form auto-populates an Excel sheet.
- Referral Excel sheet checked twice daily on all working days (start of day and mid-day)
- Double-submission process unchanged: second referral not processed unless differing information; connect with QS NS Manager if needed

What changed (processing received referrals):
- No longer sending an email to families regarding receiving their referral (CICC handles this)
- New referrals processed one at a time, oldest to newest, with the following steps per referral:
1. Copy the full row from the Excel sheet and paste into the QuickStart NS Referral Information Formatter
2. Click "Convert to Document"
3. Verify accuracy of converted document
4. Save as PDF: naming convention "QuickStart NS Referral CHILD ACRONYM"
5. Open IWK's secure email client — MOVEit
6. Click "Go to Mailbox", then "Send Package"
7. Compose email:
- To: referrals-preschoolautism@iwk.nshealth.ca
- Subject: QuickStart NS Referral CHILD ACRONYM
- Body: "Hello, Please find the attached referral"
8. Attach the PDF and send
9. Delete the PDF from your computer after sending
10. On the ANS referral form Excel sheet, put the date you sent the email to CICC in row AG

NOTE: Children age-ineligible for parent coaching but still under 45 months (e.g. ESDM program waitlist) continued as usual -- add to "Referral and Eligibility" tab. The "Referral and Eligibility" tab was generally no longer used for children under 32 months, but for children 32 months and over, processes continued as usual starting with that tab.

Consent Withdrawal Handling (Transition Period)

If referral form was submitted without consent to transfer to CICC:
- Admins to track forms submitted but not completed due to withdrawal of consent
- If contact information was provided:
- Follow up within 24 hours to complete the QS NS Referral form word document with them
- Connect with QS NS Supervisor in the zone pertaining to the child; Supervisor to connect with CICC team to arrange cohort assignment

If phone call received from family inquiring about referring to QuickStart:
- Ask: "Did you complete or try to complete the referral form on our website?"
- If yes: "Did the referral form ask you to call us directly?"
- If yes: confirm withdrawal of consent with IWK and CICC team

- If no: redirect to complete the referral form
